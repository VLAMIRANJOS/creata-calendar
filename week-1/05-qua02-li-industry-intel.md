## CAROUSEL: "Collectibles are back. And the data explains why."

### Slide 1 -- CAPA
**Headline:** Burger King reintroduced collectible toys in kids meals after 22 years. McDonald's brought back the Changeables after 39 years.

This is not coincidence. This is data.

### Slide 2 -- O FENOMENO GLOBAL
**Headline:** The collectible economy is not niche. It is mainstream.
**Body:**
The global memorabilia market reached $60 billion.

In Brazil, the pattern is even more pronounced:
- Carmed + Fini launched a collectible lip balm and generated R$40 million in 20 days.
- Hot Wheels + Formula 1/Ferrari shifted brand perception from children's toy to adult collector item -- the #1 collab in Brazil in 2025.
- Coca-Cola + Carmed created a limited charm bracelet (7,000 units) that became the most disputed item at The Town 2025.

Collectibility is not a marketing tactic. It is behavioral economics.

**Dados BR:** Carmed+Fini R$40M, Hot Wheels+F1 #1 collab 2025, Coca-Cola+Carmed The Town

### Slide 3 -- POR QUE FUNCIONA
**Headline:** The behavioral mechanics behind collectibles.
**Body:**
66% of Brazilian consumers view brand collabs positively (Orbit Data Science).

The #1 driver of success? Nostalgia -- cited in 25% of all positive mentions.

But there is a second force: scarcity + completion drive. When a consumer starts a collection, they return. Again. And again.

QSR operators know this: 66% plan to launch new value deals in 2025-2026. The brands that activate through experience -- not just price -- win.

**Dados BR:** 66% aprovacao collabs, nostalgia 25% fator #1
